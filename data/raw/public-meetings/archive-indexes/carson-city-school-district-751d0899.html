School Board

The Carson City School Board is comprised of seven members elected by the people. They serve four-year terms and are elected from individual single member districts on a staggered basis. Members annually elect their president, vice president, and clerk.

The Carson City School Board appoints and evaluates the superintendent, sets goals, adopts policies, approves the annual budget, and represents the interest of the people and schools that reside in their district.

Our District Strategic Plan was developed to intentionally guide transformational change and set the vision for where we want to be as a district over the next five years. The Strategic Plan was developed with input from staff, students, the community and business partners. Our Strategic Plan is a dynamic, active document that includes the Vision, Mission, Core Values, and Strategic Priorities for Carson City School District.

Empower Carson City 2027 Strategic Plan (English)
Plan estratégico para empoderar a Carson City 2027 (Spanish)

Vision: Our community inspires and empowers innovators, leaders, and scholars.

Mission: In partnership with families and the community, we will make the most of every day for each student by empowering them with skills , knowledge, values, and opportunities to thrive.

Strategic Plan Goals:

  1. Exceptional Staff
  2. Curriculum that Matters
  3. Engaged Parents and Families
  4. Healthy Generations of Carson Students
  5. A Community in Full Partnership

Community Connections

Purpose
The purpose of Community Connections is to work with our stakeholders in the development of district goals and objectives.

Eligibility
Parents, guardians, and community members with an interest in developing and supporting district goals and objectives.

Selection
Open to all community members

Vacancies
Not applicable

Meeting Frequency
Quarterly, 4 times per school year.
